Top 5 Chess Books for Improving Tactics (Beginners & Intermediate)

Chess tactics are essential for improving your game. Winning material through pins, forks and skewers, or even checkmating! Tactics can instantly turn the game around in your favor. Chess books are an amazing way to improve! Check out this top 5 of chess books to get better at chess tactics, and be ready to beat anyone on the chess board! Check out this book on the link below!

5.Back to Basics: Tactics by Dan Heisman

This chess book provides a clear, structured approach to tactical training. Heisman’s book focuses on the essential motifs: forks, pins, skewers, and basic mating combinations. With accessible explanations and practice exercises, it helps beginners develop confidence in spotting tactics and applying them in real games.

4. Chess Tactics for Champions by Susan Polgar

This beginner and intermediate chess book focuses on converting difficult tactical ideas into easy-to-follow lessons. Each chapter contains a specific theme with appropriate exercises. This book is highly recommended for players who want to understand why the tactics work, not just remember them.

View on Amazon

3. 1001 Chess Exercises for Beginners by Frano Masetti and Roberto Messa

1001 Chess Exercises for Beginners provides many different tactics for beginners to practise with. Think of motifs such as forks, pins, mating patterns and much more. The repetition and volume of exercises make it perfect for practising skills and tactics, and becoming instinctive. This beginner tactic book is ideal to train your tactical skills everyday.
